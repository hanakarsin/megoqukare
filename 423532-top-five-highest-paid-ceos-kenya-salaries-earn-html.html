Top Five Highest Paid CEOs in Kenya, Salaries They Earn

  • Top Kenyan executives smiled all the way to the bank in 2020 after they received KSh 1.84 billion in perks
  • However, this was a drop from KSh 1.91 billion they earned in 2019
  • Co-op Bank boss Gideon Muriuki was the highest-paid executive in 2020, taking home a total of KSh 377.3 million

Despite the COVID-19 pandemic ravaging the economy and crippling markets, the highest-paid chief executive officers in the country continued to enjoy excellent salaries and huge allowances.

The top CEOs managing multi-billion companies take home hundreds of millions annually.

According to The Standard, top executives smiled all the way to the bank in 2020, after they received KSh1.84 billion in perks, a drop from KSh 1.91 billion earned in 2020.

A report by Whoownskenya.com showed Co-operative Bank CEO Gideon Muriuki topped the list with a monthly salary of KSh 31.37 million per month.

TUKO.co.ke looked at the top five highest-paid CEOs in Kenya and the amount they earn annually.

1. Gideon Muriuki

Gideon Muriuki is the group managing director and CEO of Co-operative Bank. He earned a total of KSh 377.3 million in salaries and allowances in 2020.

The experienced banker joined the lender in 1996 as a senior corporate manager and steadily climbed up the ladder.

In 1999, he was promoted to director of corporate and institutional banking before being appointed the bank's manager in 2001 after the bank reported a loss of KSh 2.3 billion.

With a bachelor of science degree in Mathematics and an honorary doctorate in business management, he has managed to steer Co-op Bank to become one of the most profitable financial institutions.

In the first half of 2021, Co-op Bank recorded a KSh 7.4 billion after-tax profit for the second quarter of 2021, a 2.8% increase compared to the same period in 2020.

Due to the excellent performance, Muriuki pocketed a record KSh 260.3 million bonus.

He was awarded the huge amount, which was the largest in the banking industry, for protecting shareholder's returns amid the COVID-19 pandemic.

Muriuki is paid a monthly salary of KSh 31.37 million per month, making him the highest-paid CEO in Kenya.

2. James Mwangi

Mwangi rose from a simple livestock herder to head Equity Bank, one of the region's biggest banks by customer base.

According to its website as of August 2020, the lender had over 14 million customers and an asset base of $ 7.5 billion (KSh 822 billion).

In 2020, the humble banker took home KSh 278.5 million, including a bonus of KSh 4.7 million.

Mwangi guided the bank to a record KSh 17.9 billion half-year 2021 profit, a 98.4% increase from a similar period in 2020.

3. Peter Ndegwa

Peter Ndegwa, the former managing director of Diageo International Europe, officially took over the position of the chief executive officer at Safaricom, East Africa's most profitable company, in April 2020.

He became the first Kenya to head the telecommunications giant after the death of Bob Collymore.

In the 2020/2021 Financial Year ending March 2021, Safaricom's profit dropped for the first time in eight years to KSh 68.68 billion attributed to COVID-19 disruptions.

Despite the drop, Ndegwa was paid KSh 201.5 million in his first full year as Safaricom boss.

4. Andrew Cowan

The former chief executive officer of East Africa Breweries, Andrew Cowan, earned KSh 151.7 million in 2020, an increase from KSh 115.7 million in 2019.

Jane Karuku replaced Cowan in January 2021.

However, in March, he made a comeback as the group’s managing director for Africa regional markets.

5. Joshua Oigara

In terms of monthly salary, the Kenya Commercial Bank (KCB) CEO is the second-highest-paid with a monthly pay of KSh 22.75 million.

His allowances, though, in 2020, did not match that of the top executives.

After he opted to forego his bonuses, he dropped to the fifth position with an annual income of KSh 116.2 million.

This was a big drop from the KSh 299.1 million he took home in 2019.
