Slain Kiiru Boys Principal's Daughter Pleads with Court to Forgive Mother Convicted of Murder

- The daughter of slain Kiiru High School Principal June Muthoni told the court she believes her mother was innocent of the murder

- June pleaded with the court to have mercy on her mother Jane, who was found guilty of the murder on April 22

- The final student at the University of Nairobi said the absence of both her parents had made life unbearable for her and her siblings

The daughter of the late Kiiru High School Principal Solomon Mwangi has pleaded the court to be lenient with her mother, Jane Muthoni, who was found guilty of her husband's murder.

June Muthoni, the couple's first-born daughter, narrated how she and her siblings have been having a rough time because after their father died and their mother incarcerated, they were forced to turn to a relative for basic needs.

Speaking on Tuesday, May 18, during court proceedings, the final year at the University of Nairobi said she enjoyed a close relationship with her father and wanted justice for his death.

Nonetheless, she added, the absence of both her parents in her life and that of her siblings unbearable because besides their unmet financial needs, they also craved parental care and love.

"We do not have anyone we can rely on for our family needs. We have been depending on our relatives and it has been so tough. We have school fees arrears and also crave for parental love,” she said.

June has two twin sisters and a brother whom she said she has been taking care of since the imprisonment of her mother.

The young lady said she believed her mother was innocent and was being falsely accused of a crime she did not commit.

June's mother was found guilty of her husband's death on Thursday, April 22, and Justice Ngugi will deliver her sentence on June 3, 2021.

Mwangi went missing on November 4, 2016, and was found dead eight days later in a coffee plantation in Juja, Kiambu county.

Three suspects, including his wife, were arrested and arraigned days later.

Investigations led by Police Inspector Clement Mwangi further showed Muthoni received a text message from Kariuki, who was at the crime scene on that fateful day.

Kariuki was sentenced to a seven-year jail term after pleading guilty to manslaughter and consequently accepted to become a state witness in the mysterious killing.

In his submission, Kariuki had confessed the widow drove them to Mukurue, upon which his accomplice Ng'ang'a tied a rope around the victim's neck and forcibly dragged him out of the car.
