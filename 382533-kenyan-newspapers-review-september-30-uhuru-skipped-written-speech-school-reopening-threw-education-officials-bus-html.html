Kenyan newspapers review for September 30: Uhuru altered speech on school reopening, threw education

The dailies on Wednesday, September 30, widely reported on the escalating rift between President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy, William Ruto, following the latter's decision to snub the national COVID-19 conference.

The dailies also reported on the president's last minute decision to keep primary and secondary schools shut despite having been advised by education officials to reopen.

1. The Star

Treasury Cabinet Secretary Ukur Yattani has directed Public Service Commission (PSC) to stop recruitment of new employees for a period of three years.

The CS also wrote to all ministries and state departments informing them about the government's decision to stop the recruitment of new staff.

Yatani said the decision to halt the process was informed by the hurting economy and ballooning wage bill which would be strained and stretched beyond the limit if the PSC continued to recruit new employees.

2. The Standard

Deputy President William Ruto is a man on a ranting spree and appears to have drawn the line in the sand.

On Tuesday, September 29, the DP who was joined by a section of "hustlers" at his Karen residence tore into his critics whom he claimed were out of touch with the reality.

"We will continue with what we are doing wata-do (what will they do)? They will have to submit. Respect is two ways. You respect to be respected. I am a DP now but I rose from selling chicken," he said.

Ruto warned that those underrating the power of the hustlers' nation will be shocked when the right time comes.

"I am sending a notice; it is not going to be business as usual where we talk about tribes. However difficult and uncomfortable it will be, we will do it," said the DP.

3. Daily Nation

President Uhuru Kenyatta's decision to keep primary and secondary schools closed got education officials by surprise.

According to the publication, the head of state had been advised to reopen schools and a date had been agreed upon; all was indicated in his speech.

However, during his address, the president ignored what had been written and issued his own directives, throwing the officials under the bus.

He would later invite them to State House and give them the green light to start phased reopening of colleges and universities.

4. Taifa Leo

The Swahili publication takes a swipe at Deputy President William Ruto, Opposition chief Raila Odinga and Wiper party leader Kalonzo Musyoka for commencing early campaigns using same promises they failed they failed to keep when in office.

Ruto has been crisscrossing the country to woo "hustlers" to support his presidential bid in 2022 while Raila has been using the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) to keep his State House ambitions alive.

Kalonzo who has camped in Msambweni where a parliamentary by-election is set to take place in December has been using the platform to popularise his presidential bid.

5. People Daily

The publication reported on the plight of patients at Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH) following the ongoing workers' strike.

Patients who were still undergoing medication were discharged while those who arrived at the facility to seek treatment were turned away at the gate.

Reports indicate at least two patients have succumbed to different diseases since the strike that has paralysed operations at Kenya's largest health facility began on Monday, September 28.

The same dire situation was reported in Coast Provincial Hospital where workers downed tools as well.
