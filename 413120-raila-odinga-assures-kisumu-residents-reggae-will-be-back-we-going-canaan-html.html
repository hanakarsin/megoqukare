Raila Odinga Assures Kisumu Residents Reggae Will Be Back: &quot;We're Going to Canaan&quot;

- ODM leader Raila Odinga said just like Job and Jesus faced and overcame challenges in the Bible, BBI proponents will also overcome the BBI setbacks

- He also assured his supporters that the proverbial journey to Canaan was still on

- However, many Kenyans called him out for disregarding COVID-19 protocols in his Kisumu stopover

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga has assured his supporters from his Nyanza backyard that the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) 'reggae' will be back.

Raila used Biblical references, to assure Kondele residents that the landmark ruling made against BBI was just a setback which they will ultimately overcome.

Speaking during his Monday, May 24, Kisumu tour, the ODM leader said just as Job and Jesus went through tumultuous moments but emerged victorious as stated in the Bible, BBI proponents will also overcome the setbacks on their way.

"We promised you we are heading somewhere, do you remember? The journey is not easy, there is a lot that is happening on the way. If you read the Bible, Job was put to temptation by God, all his relatives died but he remained strong. Jesus went through the same predicament, satan took to the top of the hill to test his faith. We are facing similar challenges but we will deal with them(opponents). Are you getting me? Nobody can stop reggae," he said.

Disregard of COVID-19 rules

However, many people raised concerns over how he broke COVID-19 rules after attracting a huge crowd of people who were maskless and who blatantly disregarded social distancing.

Muli Kavolonza said:

"I thought public gatherings were banned! You are just helping spread COVID- 19 in Kisumu. You don't have people's lives in your heart."

Victor Mukabana wrote:

"Then boom, after two months we will be told the Indian COVID-19 variant has brought up the fourth wave. God save Kenya!"

Fred Erick Sags said:

"Is this the beginning of locking down in Kisumu? So that we don't hold Madaraka Day celebrations? COVID-19 kills."

Peter Khamala said:

"Rallies and gatherings were stopped, don't expose the innocent Kenyans to COVID-19 again. Reggae was stopped."

This came days after Siaya Senator James Orengo said Kenyans will go to the referendum once the legal challenges facing the Building Bridges Initiative process are completed.

He observed the coming weeks would be historical and political in Kenya's political life as the BBI proponents would be heading to the appellate court.

Orengo is among lawyers whose services have been sought by President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila to appeal the BBI judgment.
