Juliana Cherera Appointed IEBC Vice-Chairperson to Deputise Chebukati

  • The IEBC chairperson Wafula Chebukati said Cherera was unanimously elected to take up the vacant position
  • Cherera and three other commissioners were recently picked from 35 interviewed by the selection panel
  • The four replaced Nkatha, Roselyne Akombe, Margaret Mwachanya, and Paul Kurgat, who resigned after the 2017 polls

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) has announced the election of the agency's vice-chairperson Juliana Whonge Cherera.

The IEBC chairperson Wafula Chebukati said Cherera was unanimously elected to take up the position which was left vacant following the surprise resignation of Consolata Nkatha in 2018.

"The commission is pleased to announce to members of the public and stakeholders that commissioner Julian Whoge Cherera was unanimously elected vice chairperson of the commission in a plenary meeting held at the commission boardroom on Tuesday, September 14," Chebukati stated.

Cherera, Francis Mathenge Wanderi, Irene Cherop Masit, and Justus Abonyo Nyang’aya were recently picked from 35 interviewed by the selection panel.

The four replaced Nkatha (former vice-chairperson), Roselyne Akombe, Margaret Mwachanya, and Paul Kurgat, who resigned after the 2017 polls.

The trio resigned from the electoral body, citing a lack of confidence in the leadership of its chairperson Chebukati.

"For far too long and way too many times, the commission's chair has failed to be the steady and stable hand that steers the ship in difficult times and gives direction when needed. Instead, under his leadership, the commission's boardroom has become a venue for peddling misinformation, ground for brewing mistrust and a space for scrambling for and chasing individual glory and credit," they said in a statement then.

Akombe left the commission days before the repeat of the 2017 presidential election.

Over 700 Kenyans had sent in applications for the vacant posts of the four commissioners with 120 of the candidates being holders of doctorate degrees (PhD).

Some of the top names who had made into the final list were Environment Cabinet Secretary Keriako Tobiko's sister Naisiae Tobiko, Kenya’s ambassador to Zimbabwe Lucy Jebet Chelimo, and Kenya’s deputy permanent representative to the United Nations Koki Muli.
