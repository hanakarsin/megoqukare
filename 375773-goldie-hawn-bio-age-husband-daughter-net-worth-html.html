Goldie Hawn biography

Goldie was born on November 21, 1945 in Washington D.C. The name of her mother is Laura Hawn while that of her father is Edward Rutledge Hawn. Her mother was the owner of a jewellery store and a dance school.

Goldie's father worked as a band musician, and he used to play at major events in Washington. She has one sister named Patti Hawn. She spent most of her early childhood years in Takoma Park, Maryland. For her high school education, she attended Montgomery Blair High School.

After graduating from high school, she enrolled at American University to pursue a course in Drama. However, she did not complete her university education but dropped out to concentrate on her ballet dancing career.

Dancing career

Goldie started her career as a ballet dancer. At the age of three years, she enrolled in ballet and tap dance classes. She was part of the dance crew that danced in the corps de ballet of the Ballets Russes de Monte Carlo during the production of The Nutcracker (1995).

In 1964, she made her professional dancing debut during the production of Can-Can at the Texas Pavilion of the New York World's Fair. She began working as a professional dancer a year later and appeared as a go-go dancer in New York City and at the Peppermint Box in New Jersey.
