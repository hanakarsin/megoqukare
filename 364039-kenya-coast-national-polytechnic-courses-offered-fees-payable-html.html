Kenya Coast National Polytechnic: courses offered and fees payable

The Kenya Coast National Polytechnic (KCNP) is a learning institution dedicated to helping students upgrade their existing skills and receive technical education. As an educational centre, the Polytechnic has come a long way from being a technical high school in the 1950s to a college in 1984. Today, KCNP provides training to learners from all backgrounds and helps them to conduct research programmes that expand their knowledge and prepare them to be competitive in the working world.

The Kenya Coast National Polytechnic is one of the best institutions for students to enrol in Mombasa. As a learner, you will go through mentorship sessions and practical-based training that will empower you and improve your skills, which will be crucial towards your growth as a professional. KCNP offers many diplomas, certificate, artisan, craft, and other technical programmes.

MMUST programmes offered at KCNP

KCNP also offers curriculum studies developed by the Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology (MMUST). Under this programme, the diploma and certificate courses offered are:

  • Diploma in Community Health and Development
  • Diploma in Medical Biotechnology
  • Certificate in Community Health and Development
  • Certificate in Computerised Inventory Management for Health Professional
  • Certificate in Medical Biotechnology

For the diploma programmes at KCNP, a candidate will qualify if they have a minimum mean grade of C- (minus) or C (plain) depending on the course. Study durations are between six months and three years.

Kenya Coast National Polytechnic application form

For students that want to join KCNP, they will have to fill the Kenya Coast National Polytechnic application form. The application form is available online from the institution’s website. You can only fill it from the KCNP site and submit it for approval.

When applying online, you will have to provide your email address which the institution will use to contact you. If selected, you will use the email to submit a scanned copy of a non-refundable application fee. The fee is KES 200. You will also have to submit copies of your academic transcripts, government-issued ID card, and your KCSE or KCPE leaving certificate.

Admission letters

If your selection to KCNP for September 2020 intake is successful, you will receive the Kenya Coast Polytechnic admission letter. Here is how to get it:

  • Visit the KUCCPS section of KCNP’s website
  • Go through the list of names displayed
  • Locate your name and click on it
  • Download and print your admission form

You will also have to print other documents, including the fees structure and course requirements. Next, you must bring the admission letter and your original documents (education transcripts and certificates) when joining the institution for the first time.

Fee structure - How much will you pay?

The Kenya Coast Polytechnic fee structure varies depending on the course a student wants to undertake. Programmes with longer durations require a higher fee amount compared to those that take hours or a few months to complete. Get more information about the institution’s fee structure from its website.
