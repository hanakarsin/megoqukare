Victor Mayweather of Auntie Boss: family, facts, rise to fame

The extravagant stereotypical Luo man - Victor Mayweather of Auntie Boss, is undoubtedly one of Kenya's most respected and loved actors. Acting as Silprosa's boss and Donovan's good friend, Victor's talent and charisma have won him a massive fan base and fame across the East African region.

If you are a fan or have watched NTV's Auntie Boss, you will probably agree that he brings out the character Victor Mayweather so well. Mayweather knows how much he is worth and is not afraid to let it be known to the public. He is also the most spendthrift resident within the neighbourhood, who is not scared of showing off his women and showering them with gifts.

Not many people know who Victor Volcano Mayweather is in real life, and how he rose to fame? Below are some few notable facts about the actor you probably did not know.

1. Mayweather of Auntie Boss real name is David Opondoe

Much as he is known to many of his fans and followers as Victor Mayweather, it is not his birth name. His real name is David Opondoe. David is a professional actor, who is famously known for his flamboyant role on Auntie Boss.

He is also a director, choreographer, and a scriptwriter who has been in the entertainment industry for a long time.

2. Victor Mayweather majored in Geography and Sociology at Egerton University

David went to Muslim primary and Jamhuri Boys for his basic education and later joined Egerton University for his tertiary studies. The celebrated actor studied Geography and Sociology at Egerton University.

Nonetheless, pursuing acting was his passion and while at the university, he was the chairman of Egerton Creative Players for straight two years. The position allowed him to grow his acting career.

3. His passion for acting manifested at a young age

Like many other prominent actors and actress on and outside the continent, David Opondoe, aka Victor Mayweather discovered his passion for the theatre at a significantly younger age.

He would learn traditional Dholuo song from his grandmother during the holidays and upon the reopening of schools, he would teach his classmates.

He first appeared on Television while in primary school. While teaching his standard three classmates the traditional Dholuo songs, their performance was well received and aired on Kipindi cha Watoto on KBC.

4. David Opondoe is the managing director of Phoenix Players

Mayweather's passion for theatre landed him the lucrative position as the managing director of Phoenix Players. Phoenix Players is the largest and oldest repertory theatre company in East and Central Africa.

The company is situated in Nairobi and was registered in 1983 as a non-profit making company.

The one job he had never sought at the company was the MD's position. However, when the slot opened up after the former managing director - Ngethe Githinji resigned, and David was passing by the theatre to pick up tickets to a forthcoming show, the board invited him to take an interview for the job. He didn't hesitate.

5. David Opondoe co-starred with Lupita Nyong'o in There Goes the Bride

Other than his famous play role on NTV's Auntie Boss, Mr. Opondoe has featured in other prominent pieces at Phoenix Players theatre. He has even co-starred alongside Hollywood's all-time star Lupita Nyong'o. The two featured together in the 2002's There Goes the Bride.

6. David is a private man

David Opondoe is one man who has managed to keep his family matters away from the prying and eager eyes of the public. Unlike other famous Kenyan celebrities whose families are known, Victor Mayweather's wife and children, if there are any, are not known to many people.

Auntie Boss Storyline and characters

Auntie Boss is a Kenyan comedy-drama television series aired on NTV. The series is based on the lives of the domestic house helps in a fictional Taifa Estate, and their everyday dramas revolve around the families they work for and the activities within the neighbourhood.
