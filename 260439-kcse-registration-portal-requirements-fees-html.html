KCSE registration 2024: portal, requirements, fees, and other details

Tightened regulations on the registration of KCSE exam centers, a shortened registration timeframe, and revised criteria for repeat candidates are among the recent legislative changes impacting KCSE candidates. The Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC) has provided comprehensive guidelines for KCSE exam registration, mandating strict adherence by all sub-county and county educational directors as well as school principals. It's noteworthy that any school with fewer than 15 candidates is disqualified from registering as a KCSE examination center.

The KNEC portal is designed to be used by KCSE examination centres to register their candidates and check for KNEC results for KCSE, KCPE and colleges. In addition, these centres are obliged to access the KNEC examiners portal on www.knec-portal.ac.ke for registration and download of application forms for KCSE registration.

KCSE registration

Do you want to know how to register your exam centre for KCSE exams online? To begin, you must have more than 15 candidates to register for your school.

Applicants from schools with less than five candidates will be registered at another approved examination centre. Schools with 6 to 14 applicants will be housed at a different location chosen by the Sub County Director of Education.

Information about such schools, as well as registration forms, must be submitted to KNEC. During candidates' registration, the hosted school would preserve its code.

When is the KCSE registration deadline?

The registration of 2024 KPSEA and KCSE candidates to begin on the 29th of January to the 29th of March.

Accessing the system for KCSE registration online

How can I register for KCSE online? To register on KNEC's portal first, you must have the appropriate username and password. The username, in this case, will be the school code, which is an 8-digit number.

To get the password, you can send a text to 22262 in the format KCSE#centrecode. You will then receive a message with an 8-digit number as the login password.

You will then enter your specific username and password then click "Log in". After successfully logging in, you will be required to change the password as a security precaution, and a form will appear. This form allows you to change the password. Note that this form will only appear if you're accessing the portal for the first time.

How to change your KCSE portal password

To change your password, then follow these guidelines.

  • Key in the 8-digit number you received via SMS in the 'Current Password' textbox.
  • Create the new password in the region labelled 'New Password'.
  • Confirm your new centre's password by re-typing it in the textbox labelled 'New Password'. Do not copy-paste.
  • Click on 'Change Password' for you to effect the changes.
  • To proceed to the upload of registration details, click 'Continue'.

How to go about KNEC online registration for KCSE

The headmaster of a particular school or any authorized persons may follow the steps highlighted below.

  • Go to KNEC's website
  • Download the candidates' registration application form
  • Enter the details of the candidates for the creation of the registration file
  • Login into the website again and:
  • Upload the registration file
  • Enter "new candidate" to register the new candidate

You will also be able to edit the details of already registered candidates, view or download the school invoice, nominal roll, or photo register, and register more candidates.

Applicants can also confirm whether or not they've been registered successfully by visiting the KNEC website. Note that this confirmation won't require logging in using a username or password.

The school principals should be aware that the application is only available for periods before the registration deadline. According to the school's admissions registry, each candidate will be issued an index number.

New school registration requirements

Any new institution that wants to register candidates for KNEC exams must meet the following requirements.

  • Fill up and submit an application form for the registration of a new examination centre on the KNEC website.

KCSE registration for private candidates

KCSE private candidates and those who wish to bridge will be allowed to register for their exams by paying an enrollment fee of Ksh.2000 and a minimum fee of KSh200 per paper. Practical papers are charged at KSh.100 each.

Registration of candidates with special needs

According to KNEC, students with special needs include the following:

  • Candidates who are visually handicapped, such as those with limited vision or who are blind
  • Candidates who are deaf
  • Candidates who are physically challenged
  • Candidates that are mentally challenged

Information such as the index number, subjects, and degree of disability must be recorded throughout the registration procedure. Upon receipt of supporting evidence from a professional medical authority, the council will assist the candidates appropriately, depending on the degree of impairment.

Photograph requirements for KNEC KCSE online registration

The following guidelines should be followed while taking a candidate's photo.

  • Must be a passport-sized photo (45mm by 35mm) taken using a digital camera
  • The background should be cream or light grey.
  • The photo should be taken with eyes open and clearly visible; no sunglasses or spectacles should face straight forward with a neutral expression.
  • The photo should be clear and focused, with a significant contrast between the face and the background.
  • The candidate's whole face, neck, and shoulders must be visible.
  • The image should be current (not more than six months old).
  • All photos should be taken in school uniform.

How to upload photos for KNEC KCSE registration

To upload the photos for registration purposes, follow these steps.

  • Transfer the candidate's photos into a computer and save them using their index number and not their name.
  • Copy and paste all the photos into a new folder.
  • Zip or compress the folder, i.e. Right-click on the folder with the photos, then select "Send" and click on "Compressed (zipped) folder".
  • Login to the KNEC portal
  • Enter your user name and password
  • Once logged in, select upload candidates' photos and click continue.
  • You will be directed to your computer file manager, browse to the compressed photos folder, and click upload.
  • A message will show on the application once successfully uploaded.

KNEC Examination fees

All applicants in private and governmental institutions have their examination fees paid by the government. The following candidates, however, will be required to sponsor themselves:

  • Candidates who are not Kenyan citizens
  • Private candidates registered at private examination centres in the Sub County
  • Candidates who are taking the exam for the second time/repeaters

KCSE Qualifying Test

The KCSE Qualifying Test (QT) is a competency test that determines if a candidate is qualified to take the KCSE exam. The exam is completed in one day and is open to all bonafide residents of Kenya and those serving in Kenya missions overseas.

Documents required for the KCSE Qualifying Test registration

According to the regulations, the individuals must meet the following admission conditions:

  • All individuals who have a primary certificate that isn't a KCPE certificate, such as a Certificate of Primary Education (CPE).
  • All individuals who have completed less than eight years of primary school and have a foreign primary certificate.

How much is the KCSE Qualifying Test fee?

The registration price for the KCSE qualifying test is Ksh. 3000 per candidate. The fees should be deposited in either the National Bank, Kenya Commercial Bank, Co-operative Bank, or Equity Bank's KNEC KCSE fees collection accounts.
