Mungai Mbaya's Girlfriend Aisha Says June and July Are Her Worst Months after Losing Child

  • Mungai Mbaya's girlfriend Aisha said June and July are her most stressful months
  • Aisha said she looked forwards to spend the months indoors breastfeeding, only for her son to die
  • The young lady wondered what June and July 2022 would bring

Aisha, the girlfriend of former Junction Juniors actor Mungai Mbaya, has termed June and July 2020 as her worst months.

According to Aisha, for the past two years, the two months have brought her sadness.

Via her Instagram stories, Aisha divulged that at the beginning of 2021, she assured herself that all would be well in June and July.

However, it was never to be as she lost her one-month-old son with Mungai, the brother to former Machachari actor Tyler Mbaya alias Baha.

“I swear for the past two years, June and July have been an absolute mess for me. I remember when this year was starting, I said to myself at least this year’s June and July will find me at home chilling and breastfeeding. What could go wrong with that? Little did I know.Now I'm stuck here again,” she wrote.

The heartbroken young lady wondered what the months of June and July 2022 have in store for her.

“What will next year's June and July bring?” Aisha posed.

Aisha decries fake friends

Mungai and Aisha buried their son on June 30, 2021. Speaking afterwards, she said that their son’s unfortunate demise made her realise who her true friends are.

Via Instagram, Aisha hit out at people who seemingly pretended to care. She said many of them were not there to support them when they needed it the most.

"When I was pregnant, no one came to see me. When I had my baby, no one came to see us. Now that he's gone, everyone wants to come over,” Aisha wrote.

Aisha said few people checked up on her during the stressful period.

“No one checked up on me. Only a few were true to their words. This was a time everything in our lives was tested. Especially true friendship,” she stated.

She expressed gratitude to those who stood by them, writing:

“I'll forever love and appreciate everyone who was truly there and everyone who helped us. Everyone who was there for my son.”
