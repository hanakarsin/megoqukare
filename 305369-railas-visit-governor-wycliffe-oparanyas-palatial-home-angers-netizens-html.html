Raila's visit to Governor Wycliffe Oparanya's palatial home angers netizens

Kenyans online have expressed mixed reactions after photos of Kakamega Governor Wycliffe Oparanya's palatial home surfaced online.

Oparanya hosted Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga for lunch at his Emabole home in Butere, Kakamega county, on Sunday, May 12.

Netizens were perplexed with the photos of opulence that emerged online shortly after Raila landed at the governor's homestead which is well equipped with a helipad.

His house is well furnished with modern furniture and the living room polished with elegant carpets resembling those of a castle.

As section of Kenyans On Twitter (KOT) expressed fear that the county chief could be using taxpayers money to live such a luxurious life.

Oparanya has served in various capacities as a leader since early 1980s.

He worked as a senior audit manager for Kenya Aerotech company before being elevated as the company's financial director in 1996.

The governor also served as an MP for Butere constituency as well as minister of State for Planning, National Development and Vision 2030 between 2008 and 2013.

He was elected governor of Kakamega county in 2013 and is currently serving his second term.
