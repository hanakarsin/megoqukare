Somalia: Protests Erupt In Mogadishu after MPs Extended Farmaajo's Term by 2 Years

- Somali MPs on April 12 voted to extend President Mohamed Farmaajo's term by two years

- During the session, 149 out 275 MPs also voted to extend their mandate, with only three voting against it while one abstained

- Somalis are up in arms over the move which they termed subversion of democracy

Hundreds of enraged Somalis have taken to Mogadishu streets to protest after Members of Parliament in the Horn of African country extended President Mohamed Abdullahi Farmaajo's term by two years.

Opposition figures in Somalia were at odds with Farmaajo over the election process after lawmakers voted to extend his term despite his tenure having ended officially on February 8.

During the session on April 12, 149 out 275 MPs also voted to extend their mandate, with only three voting against it while one abstained.

This angered citizens who also trooped to social media to express their annoyance over what they termed a subversion of democracy.

TUKO.co.ke sampled some reactions:

Abdirashid Hashi said:

"Farmaajo faces uprising in #Mogadishu. He cannot silence the opposition with force. No army can subjugate a community who feel they are wronged. Reconciliation is the only option. Any delay or bloodletting weakens him."

Ahmed Abdullahi noted:

"Mogadishu tonight...The people are up in arms against Farmaajo's dictatorship. Revolution is in full effect. Farmaajo's time is up."

Amina Abdalla wrote:

"Mogadishu residents fleeing in droves to safer areas whereas the illegal president and his family enjoy asylum in America. For how long will the innocent and vulnerable continue to suffer for Farmaajo to stay in power. His dream of clinging on to power will never come true."

Farmaajo refuses to leave office

TUKO.co.ke had reported the move by the lower house of Somalia's parliament to extend Farmajo’s term in the volatile nation was dealt a blow after the House’s upper chamber swiftly rejected it.

But the 59-year-old refused to leave office out of defiance despite public protests in Mogadishu during which peaceful protesters were shot and killed by heavily armed government forces.

The United States, which is Somalia’s main donor, maintained that it will not support any term extension.

“We have stressed repeatedly that it is vital for the peace, stability, prosperity, and governance of Somalia that the Federal Government and the Federal Member States reach a consensus on the way forward for the electoral process,” said U.S. Secretary of State Antony J. Blinken.
