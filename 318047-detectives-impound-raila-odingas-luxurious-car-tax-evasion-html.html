NASA coalition denies report Raila's luxury car among those impounded over tax evasion

- This was in response to report by a section of the media indicating Raila's car was impounded by detectives

- The report claimed the vehicle was seized over fraudulent registration claims in tax evasion racket

- Other 17 vehicles mentioned in the report belonged to politicians and government officials

- Vehicles targeted by police include 64 Range Rovers, Mercedes Benz (94) and Toyotas (224)

The National Super Alliance (NASA) coalition has dismissed reports that detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) impounded ODM party leader Raila Odinga's luxurious car in an ongoing crackdown against tax evasion.

This was in response to a report by the Standard which indicated Raila's Range Rover registration number KCS 002D was among 18 others that were seized after Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) released a list of cars that were fraudulently registered.

In a statement issued on Wednesday, October 9, the coalition said Raila has never owned such a vehicle and that the report was malicious and sensational with the aim of maligning the Opposition leader's name.

"Odinga has never ever owned a Range Rover vehicle. Indeed, the paper admits in the same story that a search at the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) for registration particulars of the vehicle did not yield any results," read the statement.

The NASA coalition threatened to take legal action against the Standard accusing the media house of continuously publishing misinformation about the Africa Union (AU) envoy.

"To get to the bottom of today’s false and malicious story and to end this trend at the publication, and after lengthy consultations, Odinga has instructed A.T. Oluoch & Company advocates to act for him against the Standard newspaper," said the coalition.

The report also claimed Devolution Cabinet Secretary Eugene Wamalwa’s Range Rover that was flagged by KRA in 2016 was on the wanted list.

Others mentioned in the report included vehicles linked to three Jubilee MPs, Maoka Maore of Igembe North (KBL 088N, Range Rover), Mathias Robi of Kuria West (Toyota KCG 800J) and George Theuri of Embakasi West (Mercedes Benz KCR 565W ).

Kirinyaga woman rep Purity Ngirici's husband, Peter Ngirici, has a Range Rover registration number KCC 241P, which coincidentally was also indicated as having an alternative number of GKA702.

Flamboyant Nairobi city businessman Steve Mbogo's Range Rover which in April 2019 was found to have a similar registration number to that of a Probox KCL 830A was also included in the wanted list.

Other cars listed belong to former MPs including James Gesami who vied and lost in Nyamira gubernatorial contest and former Kasipul MP Oyugi Magwanga.

Out of the 444 cars that police are targeting, 64 are Range Rovers, 94 Mercedes Benz and 224 Toyotas.

Other makes include 22 Nissan, one Porsche, 26 Subarus, four Volkswagens, and two Volvos whose registration details are suspicious according to the DCI
