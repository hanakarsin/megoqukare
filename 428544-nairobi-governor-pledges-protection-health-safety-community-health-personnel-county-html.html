Governor Kananu Pledges to Protect Nairobi Health Workers, Community Service Providers

  • Acting Nairobi county governor Ann Kananu has pledged to protect the interests of all county health workers and community service providers
  • She affirmed her commitment to delivering quality health services to city dwellers and beyond
  • Nairobi City County has allocated KSh 100 million to be used in the implementation of the first phase of the Community Health Act of 2019

Acting Nairobi county governor Ann Kananu has pledged to ensure protection of health and safety of all community health volunteers and community health services personnel in Nairobi.

Deserve quality services

In a speech read on her behalf by the Acting County Secretary Jairus Musumba during the official, launch of the Nairobi City County Community Services Health Act 2019 at Ngei Primary School Langata sub-county, Kananu said Nairobi residents deserve quality services.

"Today marks a special day as we officially unveil this document that will work towards improving the health and well-being of Nairobi residents, which is critical in achieving universal health coverage, and delivery of quality and efficient basic health care services at the community level," Kananu said.

Training and certification of Community Health Volunteers

The acting governor pointed out that it has taken the county government four years of lobbying partners and the County Assembly Health Committee for the bill to be enacted into law.

Kananu affirmed her government's commitment to ensuring that the Act is fully implemented.

She also promised to arrange for the training and certification of Community Health Volunteers.

"Community health volunteers are a big part of ensuring we deliver services to Nairobi residents as they engage in tracing and referral of health-related cases to health facilities, conducting home visits and mobilizing community workers to adopt health promotion among many others. We will therefore ensure an increment in their allocation," she added

Appreciating Community Health Volunteer workers

The Cabinet Secretary for Health Mutahi Kagwe who was in attendance lauded Nairobi City County Assembly for recognising and appreciating Community Health Volunteer workers by passing the Act that is meant to protect them.

Kagwe urged all counties to pass a similar bill to ensure Community Health Volunteers are protected, appealing to development partners to put their focus on supporting Community Health services in the country.

Nairobi City County has allocated 100 million to be used in Phase 1 of the implementation of the Act.

The Nairobi county Community Health Act 2019 is meant to provide for the establishment and delineation of Community Health units within the county for effective and efficient delivery of community-based health services.

Story Aron Ndonyi, TUKO.co.ke correspondent
