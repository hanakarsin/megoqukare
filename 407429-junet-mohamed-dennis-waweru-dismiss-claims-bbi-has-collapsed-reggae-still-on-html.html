The Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) secretariat co-chairs Junet Mohamed, and Dennis Waweru have dismissed claims that the report was on the verge of collapsing.

The duo insisted that the constitutional amendment bill was reverberating, further denying that its secretariat had been closed down and its functions moved to the president's office.

Speaking on Tuesday, March 30, Waweru said those peddling rumours of BBI collapse were opponents of the report, which was their wishful thinking.

“I am very hopeful, that we will have a referendum by July this year, take this from me,” said Junet.

Handshake still intact

In the recent past, the BBI process has been undergoing challenges with a word on the street being it was being rocked by political differences between President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga.

However, the secretariat insisted the handshake between the two was still intact, and the leaders were in constant communication.

"The handshake between Uhuru and Raila remains solid, and the two leaders remain committed to the BBI. Their resolve of having a unified inclusive Kenya is unwavering," read their statement in part.

Junet had earlier claimed the newly created One Kenya Alliance was pushing ODM out of BBI.

"We have some small boys who have come in late and who are trying to push ODM out of BBI; I want to assure you, that will not happen, we will not walk away from BBI, which is a product of the handshake,” he said.

BBI reggae muted in parliament

TUKO.co.ke had reported the BBI reggae had been muted after the legal joint committee of parliament suspended its sittings indefinitely to curb the spread of COVID-19.

The meeting was held in compliance with Uhuru's directive on social gatherings, which barred the parliamentarians from holding meetings.

The adjournment could deal the report a blow, given it had timelines within which it was supposed to be finalised.

Elsewhere, Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya said he had given up on the push for the BBI referendum, arguing it was no longer tenable.

Oparanya said it would not be possible to conduct a referendum to amend the constitution amid the COVID-19 pandemic and the dwindling economic fortunes.
