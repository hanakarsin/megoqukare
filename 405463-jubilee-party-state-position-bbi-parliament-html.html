Jubilee Party says it will state its position on BBI before parliament

- The ruling party said submission of the memorandum was a crucial step in the constitution-making process

- The National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi received copies of the BBI draft bill and certificates of approval from over 40 county assemblies

- The proposed amendment will be forwarded to the president for assent if a majority of the members of each House approve it

The Jubilee Party will submit its memorandum on the Constitutional Amendment Bill 2020 before a joint committee of the Senate and National Assembly.

The ruling party said submission of the memorandum was a crucial step in the constitution-making process since the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) report had been approved by majority county assemblies.

"A delegation led by the national chairperson Nelson Dzuya will this morning at 11am appear before a joint comprising the National Assembly Committee on Justice, Legal Affairs and Senate Committee on Justice, Legal Affairs. The delegation is set to present its memorandum on the Constitution Amendment Bill, 2020, which is under consideration," the party stated on Thursday, March 11.

National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi had earlier confirmed receiving copies of the BBI draft bill and certificates of approval from over 40 county assemblies.

The Speaker noted the BBI being a constitutional amendment, the MPs would not be for the members to introduce new amendments.

"They cannot amend. Obviously it will take a very short time," he stated.

The BBI popularisation and adoption gathered more steam in February when over 40 county assemblies endorsed the proposed legislation.

The bill was taken to the county assemblies for approval or rejection after the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission verified over 1.4 million signatures appended to it.

At least 24 county assemblies out of the 47 were supposed to adopt the legislation before it moved to the bicameral House for further debate and voting.

The proposed amendment will be forwarded to the president for assent if a majority of the members of each House approve it.

But the same will be submitted to the people in a referendum if either House fails to pass the bill, or if the bill relates to a matter specified in Article 255 (1) of the Constitution.

Under Article 255(1), some areas of the Constitution must be subjected to a referendum if they are to be changed.

The bill may be introduced in either House and second reading of the proposed amendments done after 90 days following the first reading in that House.

It will be considered to have passed when each House pass it in both its second and third readings by not less than two-thirds of all the members of that House.

Only two county assemblies, Nandi and Baringo, rejected the bill while Uasin Gishu's 46 MCAs opted to abstain from voting.
