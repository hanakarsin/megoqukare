Uganda elections: Majority Kenyans say they'd vote for Bobi Wine, not Museveni, TUKO poll

- A poll by TUKO.co.ke on its Facebook page sought Kenyans' views on the Uganda elections set for tomorrow, Thursday, January 14

- Admitting that it was time for a change in Uganda after Museveni's 35-year tenure, many said they would vote for Bobi Wine

- Besides the two, nine more candidates are also seeking to clinch the presidency on Thursday

PAY ATTENTION: Join TUKO.co.ke Telegram channel! Never miss important updates

A majority of Kenyans have said they would vote for MP Robert Kyagulanyi alias Bobi Wine and not President Yoweri Museveni if they were to vote in the Thursday, January 14, polls.

Highlighting that it was time for a change in Uganda after Museveni's 35-year reign, many Kenyans said they would vote for Bobi Wine if they were voters in the country.

As earlier reported, the United States of America announced it will not take part in the diplomatic observation of the polls.

This is after US Ambassador in the country, Natalie E. Brown, disclosed the Uganda Electoral Commission (UEC) had denied more than 75% of the election observer accreditations requested by the US.

The government of Uganda also ordered internet service providers in the county to block all social media platforms and messaging apps.

The new directive came after social media users in the country complained of being unable to access Facebook and WhatsApp.

The ECU said photography will be restricted at polling stations as Ugandans head to the ballot on Thursday, January 14, to elect the president and MPs.
